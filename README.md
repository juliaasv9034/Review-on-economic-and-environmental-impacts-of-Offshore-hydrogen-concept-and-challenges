# Review on Economic and Environmental Impacts of Offshore Hydrogen: Concept and Challenges

This repository contains a comprehensive review paper developed at **Instituto Superior Técnico (IST)** regarding the integration of offshore wind energy with hydrogen production.

## Project Overview
The research explores the viability of the **Offshore Hydrogen** concept as a solution for decarbonization. It analyzes the transition from traditional offshore wind power to integrated systems where hydrogen is produced at sea, addressing the technical, economic, and environmental dimensions of this emerging industry.

## Key Research Areas
* **Technological Concepts:** Comparison between decentralized (platform-based) and centralized (onshore) electrolysis.
* **Economic Analysis:** Assessment of Levelized Cost of Hydrogen (LCOH), CAPEX/OPEX of offshore electrolyzers, and transport infrastructure (pipelines vs. ships).
* **Environmental Impact:** Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) considerations, including the footprint of offshore platforms and the impact of brine discharge.
* **Regulatory & Logistic Challenges:** Analysis of the current bottlenecks for large-scale deployment in the maritime space.

## Highlights from the Paper
* Detailed SWOT analysis of the offshore hydrogen value chain.
* Comparison of storage solutions (underwater salt caverns vs. pressurized tanks).
* Evaluation of the role of offshore hydrogen in the European Green Deal.

## Tools & Methodology
* **Academic Research:** Systematic literature review of state-of-the-art offshore energy technologies.
* **Data Synthesis:** Comparative analysis of economic data from leading pilot projects (e.g., Neptune, PosHYdon).
